I don't do research in a vacuum, I'm supported by a whole scientific community.

Colleagues for life!

My colleagues at UVM have enriched my scientific training and experience by teaching me about different areas of biology. So I'd like to highlight their research here.
  • Cristian Dambros, PhD taught me a lot of stats and community ecology.
  • Allyson Degrassi, PhD candidate taught me how changing environments impact rodent communities.
  • Federico Lopez-Osorio, PhD candidate constantly pushes me to think and approach biology with phylogenetic trees and it has changed my projects for the better!
  • Nabil Nasseri, PhD candidate opened my eyes to the beauty of multispecies interactions, specifically ant-hemipteran mutualisms.
  • Katie Ann Miller, PhD student showed me cool patterns of ant nutritional demands along a climate gradient.
  • Sam Alger, PhD candidate introduced me to how viruses hop between bee species!
  • Yainna Hernaiz Hernandez, Ms found that harvester ants get infected by diverse fungal pathogens.
  • Marion Weir, PhD candidate uncovered molecular mechanisms underlying how cells move, which is important for understanding cancer progression.
