Andrew D. Nguyen, Evolutionary Physiologist

About me: I am a 4th year PhD candidate at the University of Vermont . My training is guided by the two best mentors in the world: Nick Gotelli, who studies the community ecology and biogeography of carnivorous pitcher plants and ants; Sara Helms Cahan who studies the evolution of social structure. My dissertation has combined the ecological and evolutionary approaches of my co-advisors with my own interest in molecular biology to investigate mechanisms of temperature adaptation in ants.

Why do I study biology?

Life fascinates me...so my professional goal is to understand every facet of it. More specifically, I want to understand the forces that shape diversity at all levels of biological organization from genes to ecosystems.
